Grooming Guide

Simple routines and the right products for every hair type

Washing & Drying

  • Washing daily strips natural oils — 3–4 times per week is enough for most hair types.
  • Use a shampoo matched to your scalp type: oily, dry, or normal.
  • Massage the scalp with fingertips in circular motions, not nails.
  • Always follow with conditioner on the mid-lengths and ends.
  • Keep the blow dryer on medium heat and 6+ inches away from the scalp.
  • Pat dry with a towel — don't rub, it causes frizz and breakage.

Beard Care

  • Wash your beard 2–3 times a week with a dedicated beard wash, not face soap.
  • Beard oil softens coarse hair and moisturizes the skin underneath.
  • Apply balm for hold and shape when styling a longer beard.
  • Trim the neckline regularly — about two finger-widths above the Adam's apple.
  • A boar bristle brush distributes oil evenly and trains the hair direction.
  • Visit your barber every 3–4 weeks for edge work and shape maintenance.

Scalp Health

  • A healthy scalp is the foundation of healthy hair growth.
  • Exfoliate once a week with a scalp scrub to remove buildup.
  • Avoid scratching — it damages the follicles over time.
  • Dandruff that doesn't clear with anti-dandruff shampoo should be seen by a dermatologist.
  • Staying hydrated and getting enough sleep directly impacts scalp condition.

General Tips

  • Fades and tight cuts need a barber visit every 3–4 weeks to stay sharp.
  • Always warm the product between your palms before applying — never put cold wax directly on dry hair.
  • Apply product to slightly damp hair for better distribution and hold.
  • Use a comb to set direction first, then fingers to add texture.
  • Less product is almost always better — you can add more, you can't take it back.

Product Guide

Which product to use for which look

Wax

Medium hold, matte finish. Easy to rework throughout the day. The most forgiving daily option.

Best for: everyday styles

Pomade

High hold, shiny finish. Water-based versions wash out easily. Oil-based last longer but need more effort to remove.

Best for: pompadour, slick back

Clay

Strong hold, matte finish. Adds volume and texture. Works especially well on thick or coarse hair.

Best for: textured crops, undercuts

Spray

Light hold, used after styling to lock the shape in place. Good for fine hair that needs volume without weight.

Best for: finishing, fine hair
